SpaceLink · Revenue Model v2 — tiered & capped

The commission gets smaller as the deal gets bigger

A flat 15–18% works for small and mid-sized bookings, but on a large warehouse contract it becomes a number worth dodging. So the take-rate tapers by deal size and is capped at the top — keeping every deal on-platform.

The problem with a flat rate

Why we cap fees on large deals

A flat percentage looks fine until the deal is large. 15% of a $200,000/year warehouse contract is $30,000 — a number big enough that both sides are tempted to shake hands off-platform and cut SpaceLink out.

Disintermediation — parties bypassing the platform — is already flagged as SpaceLink's single biggest risk. The fee structure shouldn't make it worse.

The fix

Keep the percentage for small and mid deals, then cap the take on large ones — switching to a flat success fee plus a small monthly platform fee. The platform's cut never grows large enough to be worth avoiding, so big customers stay.

Small fee on a deal you keep beats a big fee on a deal you lose.

Flat vs capped

Where the cap earns its keep

On a large contract, the capped model takes far less per month — but it keeps a customer the flat model would likely lose.

Founding period & the open decision

First 6 months

Free promotional period

0% on both sides while we build liquidity. The full booking + escrow flow still runs at 0% so both sides learn to transact on-platform before fees switch on.

Open decision: show the fee % during the free period?

Show it → anchors future value and makes the founding discount feel real. Hide it → maximum-friction-free sign-up, but the eventual fee may feel new.

Flagged for the founding team — worth deciding before onboarding starts.
